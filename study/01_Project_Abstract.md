# Data Mining Menu Planning System

## 1. Abstract

The **Data Mining Menu Planning System** is a data-driven application designed to optimize restaurant menus by analyzing sales transactions, customer behavior, and item profitability. By leveraging machine learning algorithms such as Association Rule Mining (Apriori/FP-Growth), K-Means Clustering for customer segmentation, and Time Series Forecasting, this project provides actionable insights to restaurant owners. The system helps in identifying popular item combinations, predicting future demand, and classifying menu items based on profitability and popularity (Menu Engineering).

## 2. Introduction

The hospitality industry generates vast amounts of data daily, yet much of it remains underutilized. Traditional menu planning often relies on intuition rather than empirical evidence. This project aims to bridge that gap by providing a comprehensive analytics dashboard that transforms raw transactional data into strategic business intelligence.

## 3. Problem Statement

Restaurant owners face several challenges:
*   **Menu Engineering**: Difficulty in identifying which items are profitable vs. popular.
*   **Cross-Selling**: Missing opportunities to suggest complementary items.
*   **Inventory Management**: Overstocking or stockouts due to inaccurate demand forecasting.
*   **Customer Retention**: Lack of understanding of customer segments and their preferences.

## 4. Objectives

1.  **Analyze Sales Patterns**: visualize top-selling items, revenue trends, and category performance.
2.  **Market Basket Analysis**: Discover hidden relationships between menu items (e.g., "Customers who buy Burger also buy Fries").
3.  **Customer Segmentation**: Group customers based on purchasing behavior using RFM analysis.
4.  **Demand Forecasting**: Predict sales for the next 30 days to optimize inventory.
5.  **Menu Optimization**: Classify items into Star, Puzzle, Plowhorse, and Dog categories for strategic decision-making.

## 5. Scope

The system is designed for small to medium-sized restaurants. It processes historical sales data and provides a user-friendly interface for managers to view reports and recommendations. The current version uses synthetic data for demonstration but can be adapted to real-world POS data.
